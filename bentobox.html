How to Make a Classic Bento Box

A bento box is a Japanese single-portion, home-packed meal. A traditional bento holds rice or noodles, fish or meat, with pickled and cooked vegetables, all neatly packed in a box. It's a perfect, balanced, and visually appealing lunch.

This guide provides a sample menu for a classic Teriyaki Chicken Bento.

The Bento Box Formula

The key to a good bento is balance. Aim for this ratio:

  • 4 parts Carb: Usually rice or noodles.
  • 2 parts Protein: Chicken, fish, egg, tofu, or beans.
  • 1 part Vegetables: Steamed, blanched, or pickled.
  • 1 part Fruit/Treat: A small portion of fruit or a sweet treat.

Sample Menu: Teriyaki Chicken Bento

This bento consists of four main components. Prepare them separately before assembling.

1. Main: Teriyaki Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 boneless chicken thigh
  • 1 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tbsp Sake (or water)
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Vegetable Oil

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar to create the teriyaki sauce.
  2. Heat the oil in a small pan over medium heat. Pat the chicken dry and place it skin-side down.
  3. Cook for 4-5 minutes until the skin is crispy. Flip the chicken.
  4. Pour the teriyaki sauce mixture into the pan. Simmer, spooning the sauce over the chicken, until the sauce has thickened and the chicken is cooked through (about 3-4 minutes).
  5. Remove from heat and let it cool completely before slicing.

2. Side: Tamagoyaki (Rolled Omelet)

Ingredients:

  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Soy Sauce
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Beat the eggs in a bowl with sugar, soy sauce, and salt.
  2. Heat a rectangular (or small round) pan with a light coating of oil.
  3. Pour in a thin layer of the egg mixture. As it sets, roll it up to one side of the pan.
  4. Add a little more oil to the empty part of the pan. Pour in another thin layer of egg, making sure it flows under the first roll.
  5. Once set, roll the new layer up with the first. Repeat until all the egg mixture is used.
  6. Remove from the pan, let cool, and slice.

3. Vegetable: Blanched Broccoli & Carrots

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup Broccoli florets
  • 1/4 Carrot, cut into flower shapes (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Bring a small pot of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt.
  2. Add the broccoli and carrots. Cook for 60-90 seconds until bright green and tender-crisp.
  3. Immediately drain and run under cold water (or place in an ice bath) to stop the cooking.
  4. Drain well and pat dry.

4. Carb: Steamed Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • Optional: Furikake seasoning or a pickled plum (umeboshi)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice according to your rice cooker or pot instructions.
  2. Fluff and let it cool slightly before packing.

Bento Box Assembly

  1. Cool Everything: This is the most important rule! All food must be at room temperature or cold before packing. Packing hot food will create condensation and make the bento soggy.
  2. Start with the Carb: Pack the cooled rice into one section of your bento box. Press it down lightly. You can sprinkle furikake on top now.
  3. Add the Protein: Place your sliced Teriyaki Chicken next to the rice.
  4. Use Dividers: Use silicone cups or large lettuce leaves to separate wet and dry items. Place the blanched broccoli and carrots in a silicone cup or a small section.
  5. Fill the Gaps: Add the sliced tamagoyaki to fill in a main gap. Use small, "gap-filler" items like cherry tomatoes or snap peas to ensure nothing can move around.
  6. Finish: Add a small, sealed container for a piece of fruit (like mandarin orange slices) or a small dessert. Close the lid tightly.

Enjoy your homemade bento box lunch!
